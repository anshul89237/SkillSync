SkillSync Payment and Saga Concepts

How payment verification triggers business actions safely in a distributed system.

1. Payment Flow Overview

Create order: payment service creates gateway order for a business action.
Verify payment: signature verification confirms authenticity.
Emit event: business-action event published to messaging layer.
Consume action: downstream service performs domain update.
Notify: success or failure notification emitted.

2. Why Saga Pattern

  • Single transaction across multiple services is not possible.
  • Saga coordinates eventual consistency through events.
  • Failures trigger compensating action paths.

3. Reliability Controls

  • Idempotent consumers avoid duplicate business writes.
  • Retry and DLQ handling for failed messages.
  • Clear payment state transitions for observability.

4. States to Explain in Viva

  • CREATED: order initialized
  • VERIFIED: payment authenticity confirmed
  • SUCCESS_PENDING: awaiting business action completion
  • SUCCESS: business action completed
  • COMPENSATED or FAILED: rollback/failure handling path

5. Interview-ready explanation

We decouple payment from business mutation by emitting events after verification. This avoids tight coupling between services and improves fault isolation. If downstream action fails, compensation logic and retries preserve consistency while keeping the user informed.
